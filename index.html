About

Hi! I'm Melissa! I just finished a 12-week intensive Coding Bootcamp at Actualize Chicago, and have started volunteering as a developer for The Difference Engine, a non-profit organization that fulfills the tech needs of other non-profit organizations. After spending years working in a research laboratory, I decided it was time to switch careers and enter the field of software development! Here's my story!

  • College & Early Career

    My Humble Beginning

    I studied biomedical engineering in college and learned a little bit of coding, but hadn't had to do any programming in my first few years out of school! Lucky for me, I've found that programming is like riding a bike. I may be a bit rusty, but the skills are there somewhere!

  • 2010-2014

    My Interest is Born

    My interest in web development was first piqued when I joined the Chicago Tri Club (CTC) leadership. I was recruited to manage the Newbie Program and introduce first-time triathletes to the sport, but I ended up managing the website as well. I was eventually promoted to VP, but couldn't recruit others to help manage the site.

  • 2014-2016

    Trial Under Fire

    A month before I was elected CTC president, the website crashed permanently. I had to set up a new site and transfer our member database myself. This experience taught me a lot about web development, and I enjoyed being able to create a membership site even with limited knowledge.

  • 2016-2017

    The Decision to Change

    Mid-2016, my boss decided to pursue a new career in physical therapy, which motivated me to make a career change myself. I decided I really enjoyed the challenges of creating a new website for the tri club, so turn my sights toward software development.

Capstone Project: Racebook

A Race Review & Social Media App for Endurance Athletes.

For my Capstone project, I created a Yelp-like app for Endurance Races. This app was build using Ruby on Rails, VueJS, JQuery, PostgreSQL, Git, GitHub, and ChartsJS.

I utilized the Google Maps in VueJS to create dynamic maps that filter and zoom as users search for races. I pulled data from the International Triathlon Union (ITU) API to populate my race database with real races, pull real photos and results from past races, and show results for real individuals in my user database. ChartsJS was used to visualize the results data in a graphical format. HTML5 Geolocation and Google Maps was used to display race route data in relation to user position, and a Google Search functionality was used to incorporate a search feature in the race route map.
